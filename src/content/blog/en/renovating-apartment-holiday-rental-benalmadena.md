## Understanding Legal Requirements and Permits
Navigating Spanish regulations can be daunting for expats. Before starting any renovation for holiday rentals, it is crucial to understand the legal framework. Failing to comply can lead to fines and operational issues.

### Tourist Licence
For any property rented out for short-term holiday stays in Andalucía, a 'Licencia de Vivienda con Fines Turísticos' (Tourist Licence) is mandatory. This licence ensures your property meets specific requirements regarding safety, amenities, and registration. You must register your property with the Junta de Andalucía's Tourism Registry. This is a non-negotiable step for legal operation. You can find detailed information and apply through the official Junta de Andalucía tourism website.

### Building Permits (Licencia de Obra)
Most renovation work in Spain requires a building permit from your local town hall. In Benalmádena, the type of permit depends on the scope of your project:

*   **Licencia de Obra Menor (Minor Works Permit)**: Required for cosmetic changes, such as painting, replacing flooring, updating bathrooms or kitchens without altering the structural layout, or minor electrical and plumbing work. This is typically quicker and less expensive to obtain.
*   **Licencia de Obra Mayor (Major Works Permit)**: Necessary for structural changes, extensions, significant layout alterations, or major facade renovations. This permit requires detailed architectural plans and involves a longer approval process. ## How Much Does it Cost to Renovate an Apartment for Holiday Rental in Benalmádena?
Renovation costs vary widely based on the size of your apartment, the extent of the work, and your choice of materials. Here is a general guide for a typical 2-bedroom apartment in Benalmádena:

### Budgeting for Different Renovation Levels
*   **Cosmetic Refresh (€15,000 - €30,000)**: This includes painting, updating light fixtures, minor kitchen and bathroom updates (e.g., new taps, cabinet fronts), and new furniture. It is ideal for properties that are structurally sound but need a modern facelift.
*   **Mid-Range Renovation (€30,000 - €50,000)**: This involves more significant updates, such as replacing entire kitchens and bathrooms, new flooring throughout, updated electrical and plumbing systems, and potentially minor layout changes. This level significantly improves guest appeal and property value.
*   **Full Renovation (€50,000 - €80,000+)**: A complete overhaul, including structural changes, new layouts, full electrical and plumbing replacements, high-quality materials, and potentially custom features. This option is for properties requiring extensive work to meet modern standards or to maximise space and luxury. For larger properties or high-end finishes, costs can exceed €80,000.

### Cost Factors to Consider
*   **Size of the Apartment**: Larger properties naturally incur higher material and labour costs.
*   **Quality of Materials**: [Premium materials](/en/materiales-premium) offer durability and a luxurious feel but come at a higher price point.
*   **Labour Costs**: These vary depending on the complexity of the work and the expertise required.
*   **Permits and Licences**: Factor in fees for building permits and the tourist licence.
*   **Architect/Designer Fees**: If you opt for major structural changes or a custom design, these fees will apply.
*   **Furniture and Decor**: Do not forget the cost of furnishing and decorating the apartment to make it guest-ready.
