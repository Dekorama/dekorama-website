As temperatures rise on the Costa del Sol, pool and terrace projects are surging. In 2026, standards have shifted: **pool materials are more durable, safer, and aesthetically far more sophisticated** than just a few years ago. If you're planning to build or renovate your pool this summer, here's everything you need to know.

## Porcelain Tile: The New King of Pool Interiors

Traditional ceramic tile has given way to **large-format technical porcelain** for pool interiors and coping. The reasons are clear:

- **Water absorption < 0.5%** (class BIa per EN 14411 standard), making it virtually waterproof.
- **Resistance to chemical agents**: chlorine, salt, and maintenance products don't degrade it.
- **Large formats** (60x120 cm, 75x150 cm) that reduce grout joints and simplify cleaning.
- **Anti-slip finishes R11/R12** certified for wet areas and pool edges.

![Outdoor pool with large-format porcelain tile cladding](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1540541338-a2e85e1b2813?w=800&q=80)

## Glass Mosaic: The Classic That Never Dies

**Glass mosaic** remains the premium choice for pool interiors. In 2026, the **2.5x2.5 cm and 5x5 cm formats** in ocean blue, turquoise, and off-white stand out. Its advantages:

- Reflects light and creates that sought-after crystalline water effect.
- Easy to mold into curves and organic shapes.
- Completely inert to pool chemicals.
- Update: **gradient-effect mosaics** (from deep blue to light turquoise) are this summer's star trend.

![Blue glass mosaic in luxury pool](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1575429198097-0414ec08e8cd?w=800&q=80)

## Safety Standards That Are Non-Negotiable

In Spain, pool cladding must meet specific regulations. Key points for 2026:

### Anti-Slip Requirements
Standard **UNE-EN 13451** and PNBE recommendations require a **minimum R11 class** on edges, stairs, and access areas. In 2026, more projects are opting for **R12** for greater safety, especially in community or hotel pools.

### Pool Interior Color
A **white or light blue pool interior** remains mandatory in collective-use pools to ensure visibility. Private pools have more freedom: sand-toned, stone grey, or sage green interiors create a very modern natural effect.

### Chemical and Freeze-Thaw Resistance
Pool tiles must pass **freeze-thaw cycling** tests (EN ISO 10545-12 standard) — essential in inland Andalusia where winter temperatures can drop significantly.

## Color and Design Trends in 2026

![Modern pool terrace with natural-toned tiles](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1613977257363-707ba9348227?w=800&q=80)

### The "Natural Mediterranean" Palette
Colors dominating this summer:
- **Off-white and cream**: classic and timeless, amplifies the sense of light.
- **Midnight blue and cobalt**: sophisticated for designer pool interiors.
- **Sage green and olive**: the year's biggest trend, closely tied to natural landscaping.
- **Terracotta and rust**: for terrace areas seeking to integrate the Mediterranean surroundings.

### Natural Stone Effect
Porcelain tiles imitating **travertine, slate, or limestone** are highly sought-after for pool coping and surrounds. They add visual warmth and integrate seamlessly with gardens and terraces.

### Elongated Rectangular Formats (Slab)
The **slab or plank format** (20x120 cm, 30x90 cm) applied horizontally creates visual lines that elongate the space and give a very architectural look. This works especially well in *infinity* or overflow-style designer pools.

## Terrace Paving: The Extension of the Pool

The terrace surrounding the pool is just as important as the pool interior. Key points for 2026:

- **2 cm thick exterior porcelain**: the new generation of outdoor paving resists furniture, intense heat, and rain without cracking.
- **Flamed or bush-hammered finish**: better grip underfoot when wet, without sacrificing elegance.
- **Visual continuity**: using the same material on the terrace and pool coping creates a mirror effect and visually enlarges the space.
