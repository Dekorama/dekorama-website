## What Does a Full Apartment Renovation in Torremolinos Involve?
A 'reforma integral' goes beyond cosmetic changes. It's a complete transformation of your living space, often addressing underlying issues and enhancing functionality and aesthetics. This type of renovation is particularly common in older properties in areas like Torremolinos, where apartments might require significant upgrades to meet modern standards and preferences.

Key areas typically covered in a full apartment renovation include:

*   **Demolition and structural work**: Removing non-load-bearing walls to create open-plan spaces or reconfigure layouts.
*   **Plumbing and electrical systems**: Updating old pipes and wiring to improve safety, efficiency, and accommodate new appliances.
*   **Kitchen renovation**: Installing new cabinets, countertops, appliances, and fixtures. This is often a focal point of any renovation.
*   **Bathroom renovation**: Replacing sanitary ware, tiling, showers, and vanity units. Often involves reconfiguring the space for better use.
*   **Flooring**: Replacing existing floors with new tiles, laminate, or wood throughout the apartment.
*   **Wall finishes**: Plastering, painting, and potentially adding decorative elements.
*   **Doors and windows**: Upgrading to more energy-efficient or aesthetically pleasing options.
*   **Lighting**: Installing new lighting fixtures and designing a comprehensive lighting plan.

## Key Factors Influencing Your Renovation Budget
Understanding what drives renovation costs helps you plan effectively and make informed decisions. Several elements directly impact the final price of your full apartment renovation in Torremolinos.

### Size and Layout of the Apartment
Naturally, a larger apartment will cost more to renovate due to the increased volume of materials and labour required. Furthermore, significant changes to the apartment's layout, such as moving walls or reconfiguring rooms, will incur higher costs due to structural considerations and additional labour.

### Quality of Materials
The choice of materials is one of the biggest cost drivers. Opting for [premium materials](/en/materiales-premium) like high-grade porcelain, natural stone, bespoke cabinetry, or designer fixtures will significantly increase your budget compared to standard options. Dekorama offers a wide range of materials to suit various styles and budgets.

### Scope of Work
Are you simply updating surfaces, or are you planning a complete overhaul including new plumbing and electrical systems? The more extensive the work, especially if it involves moving utilities or making structural alterations, the higher the cost. A full 'reforma integral' always involves a comprehensive scope.

### Kitchen and Bathroom Renovations
These two areas are consistently the most expensive rooms to renovate due to the concentration of plumbing, electrical work, tiling, and specialised fixtures. Investing in [custom kitchens](/en/cocinas-a-medida) or [complete bathroom renovations](/en/banos-completos) can greatly enhance your property's value and your daily living experience, but they come with a higher price tag.

### Permits and Fees
In Spain, any significant renovation requires a building permit (Licencia de Obra) from the local town hall (Ayuntamiento). For a full renovation, you will typically need a *Licencia de Obra Mayor* (major works permit), which involves architectural plans and technical supervision. These permits incur fees and can take time to process. Your renovation company should assist with this process. You can find general information on building permits in Spain from official sources like the Spanish Ministry of Public Works or legal guides on property renovation.

## How Long Does a Full Apartment Renovation Take in Torremolinos?
The timeline for a full apartment renovation in Torremolinos is as important as the budget. While every project is unique, most comprehensive renovations take between 8 and 16 weeks from start to finish. This duration can be influenced by several factors:

*   **Permit approval**: Obtaining the necessary permits from the Torremolinos Ayuntamiento can take several weeks or even months, depending on the complexity of the plans and the town hall's workload. Work cannot commence until permits are granted.
*   **Scope and complexity**: A renovation involving extensive structural changes or bespoke elements will naturally take longer than one focused on surface-level updates.
*   **Material availability**: Delays in sourcing specific tiles, kitchen units, or other materials can extend the project timeline. Planning ahead and ordering materials well in advance is crucial.
*   **Contractor efficiency**: An experienced and well-organised renovation company like Dekorama can manage the project efficiently, coordinating trades and minimising downtime.
*   **Unforeseen issues**: Older properties, common in Torremolinos, can sometimes reveal unexpected issues like outdated wiring, hidden damp, or structural problems once demolition begins. These can add time and cost to the project.

## Navigating Permits and Regulations for Expats
For expats and international property owners, understanding Spanish renovation permits can be daunting. The *Licencia de Obra Mayor* is mandatory for full renovations that involve structural changes, significant layout modifications, or changes to the building's exterior. This permit requires detailed plans drawn up by a qualified architect or technical surveyor (aparejador).

Your chosen renovation company should have a deep understanding of local regulations in Torremolinos and throughout the Costa del Sol. They will typically handle the permit application process on your behalf, ensuring all documentation is correctly submitted to the Ayuntamiento. It is crucial to work with a reputable firm to avoid legal issues or fines for unpermitted work.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need an architect for a full renovation?
Yes, for a 'reforma integral' involving structural changes or significant layout modifications, you will need an architect or a technical surveyor (aparejador) to draw up plans and oversee the project. This is a legal requirement for obtaining a *Licencia de Obra Mayor* from the local town hall.

### Can I live in the apartment during the renovation?
It is generally not advisable to live in the apartment during a full renovation. The process involves significant dust, noise, and disruption, including periods without water, electricity, or functional kitchen/bathroom facilities. It is safer and more comfortable to arrange alternative accommodation.

### What's the difference between a "reforma integral" and a partial renovation?
A 'reforma integral' is a complete overhaul of an apartment, addressing all major systems, finishes, and often the layout. A partial renovation focuses on specific areas or elements, like just a bathroom or new flooring, without touching the entire property or major structural components.
