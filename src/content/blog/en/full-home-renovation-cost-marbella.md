A full home renovation in Marbella represents a significant investment and a transformative project for your property on the Costa del Sol. Understanding the potential costs involved is crucial for effective planning and budgeting, whether you are an expat resident or an international property owner.

The exact price of a complete overhaul varies widely, influenced by factors such as property size, the quality of materials chosen, and the complexity of the design. ## Quick answer

A full home renovation in Marbella typically costs between €800 and €1,800 per square metre, with luxury finishes pushing prices higher. Projects usually take 3 to 8 months to complete, depending on scope and size, making careful planning essential for properties across the Costa del Sol.

## What Influences the Cost of a Full Home Renovation in Marbella?

Several key factors determine the final price tag of a complete property renovation in Marbella. Understanding these elements will help you set a realistic budget and make informed decisions throughout the process.

### Property Size and Type

Naturally, a larger property will incur higher renovation costs due to the increased volume of materials and labour required. Renovating a small apartment will be less expensive than a large villa. The type of property also matters; an older property may require more extensive structural work, plumbing, and electrical upgrades compared to a newer build, adding to the overall expense.

### Scope of Work and Desired Finishes

The extent of the renovation is a primary cost driver. A cosmetic update, such as painting and new flooring, is far less costly than a complete overhaul involving structural changes, reconfiguring layouts, or adding extensions. Your choice of finishes also plays a major role. Opting for standard, good quality materials will keep costs moderate, while high-end, bespoke, or designer finishes can significantly increase the budget. For example, installing [custom kitchens](/en/cocinas-a-medida) with premium appliances will be more expensive than a standard kitchen.

### Materials Selection

Materials account for a substantial portion of the renovation budget. The range of options is vast, from economical to ultra-luxury. For instance, choosing [porcelain tiles in Málaga](/en/porcelanicos-malaga) from a premium collection will cost more than standard ceramic tiles. Similarly, selecting high-end taps and fittings, bespoke joinery, or advanced smart home technology will elevate the total cost. Dekorama offers a wide selection of [premium materials](/en/materiales-premium) to suit various budgets and aesthetic preferences.

### Labour and Professional Fees

Skilled labour is essential for a quality renovation. The cost of architects, interior designers, project managers, and various tradespeople (plumbers, electricians, builders, tilers) will be included in your overall budget. Reputable professionals on the Costa del Sol command fair rates for their expertise and experience. Ensure your chosen company provides a transparent breakdown of labour costs.

### Permits and Licenses

In Spain, most significant renovation projects require municipal permits, known as *licencia de obra*. These permits come with fees that vary by municipality and the scope of work. Ignoring these can lead to fines and project delays. Your renovation company should guide you through this process, ensuring all necessary paperwork is filed correctly with the local *Ayuntamiento*.

### Unexpected Issues and Contingency

Renovations, especially in older properties, often uncover unforeseen problems like hidden damp, outdated wiring, or structural issues. It is always wise to allocate a contingency fund, typically 10-15% of the total budget, to cover these unexpected expenses without derailing your project or budget.

## Typical Cost Breakdown for a Full Renovation in Marbella

While exact figures depend on your specific project, here is a general guide to what you can expect for a full home renovation in Marbella, based on a per-square-metre estimate:

*   **Basic Renovation (Good Quality Standard): €800 - €1,200 per square metre.** This typically includes new flooring, painting, updated kitchens and bathrooms with mid-range materials, and basic electrical/plumbing updates without major layout changes.
*   **Mid-Range Renovation (High Quality Finishes): €1,200 - €1,800 per square metre.** This level allows for higher quality materials, more extensive changes to layouts, custom elements in kitchens and bathrooms, and potentially some minor structural modifications.
*   **Luxury Renovation (Premium Finishes & Bespoke Design): €1,800+ per square metre.** This category involves top-tier materials, designer fixtures, advanced home automation, significant structural alterations, and highly customised designs, often requiring an architect and interior designer.

To illustrate further, consider these typical cost allocations within a full renovation:

*   **Kitchen Renovation:** A [custom kitchens](/en/cocinas-a-medida) project can range from €8,000 for a compact, functional space to €30,000+ for a large, high-end kitchen with premium appliances and bespoke cabinetry.
*   **Bathroom Renovation:** [Complete bathroom renovations](/en/banos-completos) typically cost between €4,000 and €15,000 per bathroom, depending on size, fixtures, and tile choices.
*   **Flooring:** Installation costs for new flooring can range from €30-€100 per square metre, varying significantly based on the material chosen (e.g., laminate, ceramic, porcelain, natural stone, wood).
*   **Electrical and Plumbing:** Rewiring and replumbing an entire property can cost €5,000-€20,000+, depending on the property size and the extent of the work required to meet modern standards.
*   **Painting and Plastering:** Expect to pay €15-€40 per square metre for professional painting and plastering, including surface preparation.
*   **Windows and Doors:** Replacing all windows and doors can be a significant expense, ranging from €500 to €2,000+ per unit, depending on material, glazing, and security features.

## How Long Does a Full Home Renovation Take in Marbella?

The duration of a full home renovation in Marbella is another critical aspect to consider. While every project is unique, here are some general timelines:

*   **Small Apartment (60-80 sqm):** 3-5 months
*   **Medium-Sized Apartment/Townhouse (80-150 sqm):** 4-7 months
*   **Large Villa (150+ sqm):** 6-12+ months

These estimates include time for planning, obtaining permits, material procurement, and the actual construction work. Factors that can extend the timeline include:

*   **Permit Processing:** Local *Ayuntamientos* can take weeks or even months to approve renovation permits.
*   **Material Lead Times:** Specialised or imported materials may have long delivery times.
*   **Complexity of Design:** Highly custom or intricate designs require more time for execution.
*   **Unforeseen Issues:** Discovering structural problems or hidden damage can add weeks to the schedule.
*   **Weather:** While the Costa del Sol enjoys excellent weather, heavy rain can occasionally delay outdoor work.
