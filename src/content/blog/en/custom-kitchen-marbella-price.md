## How Much Does a Custom Kitchen Cost in Marbella?
The price of a custom kitchen in Marbella varies significantly based on several factors. There is no single fixed price, as each project is unique. However, we can provide a general range to help you set expectations.

For a small to medium-sized kitchen (around 10-15 square metres) with good quality, durable materials and mid-range appliances, you might expect to pay between €15,000 and €25,000. This budget allows for a stylish and functional space that significantly upgrades your home.

Larger kitchens (15-25 square metres) or those incorporating higher-end finishes, bespoke cabinetry, and premium integrated appliances typically range from €25,000 to €40,000. This level of investment brings a luxury feel and advanced functionality.

For expansive kitchens, open-plan designs, or those demanding the absolute best in design, materials, and smart home integration, costs can easily exceed €40,000, reaching upwards of €50,000 or more. These projects often involve complex layouts, top-tier brands, and intricate custom details.

These figures include design, materials, manufacturing, and installation. Keep in mind that unexpected structural work or extensive plumbing/electrical modifications can add to the overall cost.

## What Influences the Price of a Custom Kitchen?
Understanding the components that contribute to the overall cost allows you to make informed choices and prioritise your budget. Here are the main factors:

### Cabinetry and Finishes
Cabinetry is often the largest expense in a custom kitchen. The material, construction, and finish you choose will have a major impact. Options range from durable laminates and MDF with various finishes to solid wood, veneers, and high-gloss lacquers. Solid wood or bespoke, hand-finished cabinetry will be at the higher end of the spectrum, offering exceptional durability and a luxurious appearance. The complexity of the design, such as curved units or intricate storage solutions, also adds to the cost.

### Worktops (Countertops)
Worktops are a focal point and a heavily used surface. Popular choices on the Costa del Sol include:

*   **Laminate**: Most affordable, wide range of colours and patterns.
*   **Compact Laminate**: Thinner, more durable than standard laminate, good value.
*   **Quartz**: Highly durable, non-porous, available in many colours, mid to high price range.
*   **Granite**: Natural stone, unique patterns, very durable, requires sealing, mid to high price range.
*   **Porcelain**: Extremely hard-wearing, heat and scratch resistant, ideal for modern designs, high price range. Our selection of [porcelain tiles in Málaga](/en/porcelanicos-malaga) includes options suitable for worktops.

Thicker slabs, complex edge profiles, or integrated sinks will increase the price.

### Appliances
Appliances can account for a significant portion of your budget. Integrated appliances, such as built-in ovens, induction hobs, dishwashers, and refrigerators, typically cost more than freestanding models but offer a seamless, streamlined look. Smart appliances with advanced features also command a higher price. Choosing energy-efficient models can save money in the long run, but their initial cost might be higher.

### Layout and Size
The overall size of your kitchen and the complexity of its layout directly affect the cost. A larger kitchen naturally requires more materials and labour. A more intricate design with many corners, bespoke units, or a large island will involve more detailed planning, manufacturing, and installation work, increasing the price compared to a simpler, linear layout.

### Installation and Labour
Professional installation is crucial for a custom kitchen. Skilled craftsmen ensure everything fits perfectly, is level, and functions correctly. Labour costs include demolition of the old kitchen, plumbing adjustments, electrical work, plastering, painting, and the precise fitting of all new components. The expertise of the installation team ensures the longevity and quality of your investment.

### Additional Features
Beyond the basics, many elements can enhance your custom kitchen but also add to the cost. These include:

*   **Lighting**: Integrated LED strips, pendant lights, under-cabinet lighting.
*   **Storage Solutions**: Pull-out pantries, corner carousels, drawer organisers.
*   **Smart Home Integration**: Voice-controlled lighting, smart taps, connected appliances.
*   **Flooring and Wall Finishes**: High-quality [premium materials](/en/materiales-premium) like large-format porcelain tiles or natural stone for floors and splashbacks.

## Navigating Permits and Regulations on the Costa del Sol
When undertaking a kitchen renovation in Marbella, it is important to be aware of local regulations regarding building permits. Most kitchen renovations, especially those involving changes to plumbing, electrical, or structural elements, will require an *obra menor* (minor works) permit from your local *Ayuntamiento* (town hall). For example, if you are doing [renovations in Fuengirola](/en/reformas-fuengirola) or any other municipality, the process will be similar.

It is always advisable to consult your local *Ayuntamiento* or a legal professional familiar with local planning laws before starting any significant renovation work. ## Frequently asked questions

### What is the typical timeline for a custom kitchen renovation?
A custom kitchen renovation typically takes between 6 to 12 weeks from the initial design consultation to final installation. This timeframe can vary based on the complexity of the design, material availability, and the extent of structural changes required.

### Do I need a permit for a kitchen renovation in Marbella?
Yes, most kitchen renovations involving changes to plumbing, electrical systems, or layout require an *obra menor* (minor works) permit from the Marbella *Ayuntamiento*. It is essential to check local regulations to ensure compliance.

### What is the difference between a custom kitchen and a modular kitchen?
A custom kitchen is designed and built specifically for your space and needs, offering unique dimensions and features. A modular kitchen uses pre-fabricated units in standard sizes, offering less flexibility but often at a lower cost.

### Can I use my existing appliances in a new custom kitchen?
Yes, in many cases, you can integrate your existing appliances into a new custom kitchen design. Our designers will assess your appliances and incorporate them seamlessly into the new layout, ensuring they fit both aesthetically and functionally.
