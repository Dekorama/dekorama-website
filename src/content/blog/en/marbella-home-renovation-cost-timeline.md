## What Factors Influence Full Home Renovation Costs in Marbella?
The cost of a full home renovation in Marbella is not a fixed figure. Several key factors contribute to the overall budget, and understanding these will help you prepare financially.

### Scope of Work
The most significant cost driver is the extent of the renovation. A cosmetic update, such as new paint and flooring, will be far less expensive than a complete overhaul involving structural changes, reconfiguring layouts, or adding extensions. Major works like moving walls, installing new windows, or updating electrical and plumbing systems significantly increase costs.

### Material Choices
Your selection of materials has a direct impact on the budget. Opting for standard finishes will keep costs down, while choosing [premium materials](/en/materiales-premium) like high-end [porcelain tiles in Málaga](/en/porcelanicos-malaga), bespoke kitchen cabinetry, or designer bathroom fittings will elevate the price. Dekorama offers a wide range of quality materials to suit various budgets and styles.

### Property Age and Condition
Older properties, common in many parts of the Costa del Sol, often come with hidden costs. They may require extensive updates to plumbing, electrical systems, and insulation to meet modern standards. Addressing unforeseen structural issues or damp problems can add considerable expense and time to the project.

### Labour and Professional Fees
Skilled labour in Marbella and across the Costa del Sol commands appropriate rates. The cost includes not only the builders but also electricians, plumbers, carpenters, and other specialists. Additionally, professional fees for architects, technical surveyors, and project managers can account for 10-15% of the total construction cost. These professionals ensure compliance with local building codes and manage the project efficiently.

### Permits and Licenses
Securing the necessary permits from the Marbella Town Hall (Ayuntamiento) is mandatory for most renovations. The type of permit required, such as *Licencia de Obra Menor* (minor works) or *Licencia de Obra Mayor* (major works), depends on the scope of your project. Fees for these permits vary and must be factored into your budget. You can find more information on urban planning regulations on the Marbella Ayuntamiento website.

## How Long Does a Full Home Renovation Take in Marbella?
The timeline for a full home renovation in Marbella is as variable as the cost. A realistic timeframe is essential for planning your stay or managing rental income expectations. Here is a typical breakdown:

### Planning and Design Phase
This initial stage involves consultations with architects and designers, finalising plans, selecting materials, and obtaining quotes. It can take 4-8 weeks, sometimes longer if extensive design work or multiple revisions are needed. Permit applications follow this, and approval from the Marbella Ayuntamiento can take anywhere from 4 weeks to several months, depending on the complexity of the project and the local authority's workload.

### Demolition and Structural Work
Once permits are secured, demolition begins. This phase, along with any structural modifications like wall removals or extensions, typically lasts 2-4 weeks. The duration depends heavily on the scale of the changes.

### Installations (Plumbing, Electrical, HVAC)
Rough-ins for new plumbing, electrical wiring, and heating/cooling systems are critical. This stage usually takes 3-6 weeks, often running concurrently with other structural work. It requires careful coordination among different trades.

### Finishing Touches
This is where your vision truly comes to life. It includes plastering, flooring installation (such as [porcelain tiles in Málaga](/en/porcelanicos-malaga)), painting, fitting [custom kitchens](/en/cocinas-a-medida), and completing [complete bathroom renovations](/en/banos-completos). This phase is typically the longest, lasting 6-12 weeks, as it involves many detailed tasks and specialist installations.

### Inspections and Handover
Final inspections ensure all work complies with regulations. Any 'snagging list' items, minor imperfections, are addressed during this period. This final stage usually takes 1-2 weeks before the project is officially handed over.

In total, a comprehensive full home renovation on the Costa del Sol can take anywhere from 3 to 8 months from the start of construction, not including the initial planning and permit application period.

## Estimating Your Renovation Budget: A Practical Guide
To provide a clearer picture, here are example cost ranges per square metre for different renovation levels in Marbella:

### Basic Renovation: €800-€1,000 per sq metre
This typically covers cosmetic updates: new paint, basic flooring, updated light fixtures, and minor kitchen or bathroom refreshes without changing layouts or major installations. It is suitable for properties that are structurally sound and only need a modern facelift.

### Mid-Range Renovation: €1,000-€1,250 per sq metre
This level includes more significant changes, such as new kitchens and bathrooms, some internal layout modifications, updated plumbing and electrical systems, and mid-range quality materials. It offers a substantial upgrade to the property's functionality and aesthetics.

### High-End/Luxury Renovation: €1,250-€1,500+ per sq metre
For those seeking premium finishes and extensive transformations, this budget allows for significant structural alterations, high-quality bespoke materials, advanced home automation systems, and designer fixtures. This level often involves a complete redesign and a focus on luxury and customisation.

### Don't Forget the Contingency Fund
Always allocate an additional 10-15% of your total budget as a contingency fund. This buffer is crucial for covering unforeseen issues that often arise during renovations, especially in older properties. It helps prevent project delays and financial stress.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need an architect for a full renovation in Marbella?
Yes, for any renovation involving structural changes, significant layout modifications, or extensions, an architect or technical surveyor is legally required in Spain. They design the project, ensure compliance with local building codes, and oversee the work, which is crucial for obtaining the necessary *Licencia de Obra Mayor*.

### What is the difference between *Obra Menor* and *Obra Mayor* permits?
*Licencia de Obra Menor* (minor works permit) is for simple, non-structural renovations like painting, flooring, or minor bathroom updates. *Licencia de Obra Mayor* (major works permit) is for significant projects involving structural changes, extensions, or major layout alterations, requiring an architect's involvement and more extensive approval processes.

### Can I live in my property during a full renovation?
While technically possible for minor renovations, it is generally not recommended for a full home renovation. The presence of dust, noise, lack of essential services like water or electricity, and safety concerns make it impractical and uncomfortable. Planning for alternative accommodation is advisable for the duration of the project.

### How can I ensure my renovation stays on budget?
To keep your renovation on budget, define your scope clearly from the start, get detailed quotes from multiple contractors, and avoid making significant changes once work begins. Always include a contingency fund of 10-15% for unforeseen expenses. Regular communication with your project manager is key to tracking costs.
