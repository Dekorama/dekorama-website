## Navigating Permits and Regulations for Renovations in Spain

Undertaking a renovation in Spain, including Benalmádena, often requires municipal permits. The type of permit needed depends on the scope of your project. Minor works (obra menor) like painting or changing floor tiles typically require a simpler permit, while major structural changes or extensions (obra mayor) involve more extensive applications and technical projects.

It is crucial to understand these requirements to avoid fines or delays. We recommend consulting with your chosen renovation company or the local Benalmádena *Ayuntamiento* (town hall) for specific guidance. You can find general information on Spanish building regulations through official government portals or legal firms specialising in Spanish property law, such as this guide on [Spanish property law](https://www.spanishpropertyinsight.com/guides/spanish-property-law-guide/) from Spanish Property Insight.

### What types of materials are most popular for renovations on the Costa del Sol?
Porcelain tiles are extremely popular due to their durability, low maintenance, and wide range of styles suitable for the climate. ### Do I need a permit to renovate my property in Benalmádena?
Yes, most renovations in Benalmádena require a permit, known as a *licencia de obra*. The specific type of permit depends on the scale of the work. Minor cosmetic changes might need a *licencia de obra menor*, while structural alterations or extensions will require a more comprehensive *licencia de obra mayor*. Always check with the local town hall or your renovation company.

### Is it better to buy materials separately or from a full-service company?
