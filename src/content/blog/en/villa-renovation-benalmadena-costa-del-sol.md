## Navigating Permits and Regulations in Spain
For any significant renovation project in Spain, obtaining the correct permits is not just a formality; it is a legal requirement. Ignoring this can lead to fines, forced demolition, or difficulties when selling your property. In Benalmádena, you will primarily deal with the local town hall (Ayuntamiento).

Key permits and considerations include:

*   **Licencia de Obra Menor:** For minor renovations that do not affect the structure or exterior of the building. Examples include painting, changing flooring, or updating a bathroom without layout changes.
*   **Licencia de Obra Mayor:** Required for major structural changes, extensions, significant layout alterations, or any work affecting the building's facade. A technical project signed by an architect or technical engineer is mandatory for this.
*   **Occupancy Licence (Licencia de Primera Ocupación):** For new builds or major renovations that significantly alter the property's use or structure, a new occupancy licence may be required before you can legally inhabit the property.

The process typically involves submitting detailed plans, technical reports, and various forms to the urban planning department (Urbanismo) of the [Benalmádena Town Hall](https://www.benalmadena.es/). It is highly recommended to engage a local renovation company or architect who understands these procedures. Dekorama has extensive experience navigating these regulations for [renovations in Marbella](/en/reformas-marbella), Fuengirola, and across the Costa del Sol, ensuring your project remains compliant.

For official information on Spanish building regulations, you can refer to the Código Técnico de la Edificación (CTE), which sets out the basic quality requirements for buildings. While the full document is in Spanish, understanding its existence is key. Consulting with local authorities or a professional is always the best approach.
